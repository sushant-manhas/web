🏡Our prediction of 2026 housing market

Consensus is right on the average. Wrong on the spread.

You've read the headlines: 2026 is a flat year for housing, up 1–2% nationally.

True on average. False on your block. The two markets the average hides are moving in opposite directions. Your zip code decides which one your house is in.

Why

Three reasons. Together they explain every number above.

Reason 1: The lock-in cage holds

Your neighbor is not selling. The median homeowner has held their loan 12 years, double what it was 20 years ago. The math does not work for them at today's rate.

Reason 2: Same country, opposite outcomes

Hartford is up. Austin is down. Same country. Same rates.

The split is not isolated to extremes. It runs across the entire map.

Reason 3: Rates stay stuck. The split holds.

If you own in Chicago, New York, Philadelphia, Detroit, or Boston, expect another year of equity growth.

If you own in Tampa, Miami, Dallas, Denver, or Austin, expect another down year. Declines shrink. They do not stop.

Listings only loosen when mortgage rates fall under 5.5%. They do not fall there in 2026. Inventory in your market stays tight.

Your address determines your 2026.
Where We Differ

21 of 24 major forecasters expect prices to rise in 2026. Consensus: +1.43%.

Our number sits inside that range. The split does not. Consensus anchors on the national average. You do not live there. The spread tells you forecasters disagree. It does not tell you which side your zip code falls on. We do.

Builders are bracing. Watch what they are doing in your market.

This call breaks if any of three things happens. Mortgage rates fall under 5.5%. A wave of new listings hits the market. Builders cut prices deep enough to drag resale down with new construction. None of those happens this year.

Expect home prices to keep rising in 2026, in the cities nobody is watching.
